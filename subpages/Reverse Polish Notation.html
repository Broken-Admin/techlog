Reverse Polish Notation

A different way of writing arithemtic expressions.

Return to Catalog

Reverse Polish notation is a way of writing algebraic expressions.

It contrasts in comparison to the commonly used infix notation, which places the operators - such as +, -, or * signs - between operands, reverse Polish notation has the operators placed after the operands.

For example, the writing of the same equation can be compared, in infix notation 1 + 2 is written to convey the addition of 2 plus 1. In RPN - reverse Polish notation - this same expression could be written as 1 2 +

This can be used to more easily express the stack data structure, in which numbers must be pushed - put on top of the stack - before the operation can be performed upon them.

Such as 1 2 + being interpreted as pushing 1, then 2, to the stack. Then popping - taking them off of the top of the stack - to perform the addition operation upon them, then pushing the result to the top of the stack.
