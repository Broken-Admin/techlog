Similarity and Rules

French is in the Romance Language family, alongside Spanish, Italian, Portuguese and others.

The Romance Languages stem from "vulgar" - nonstandardized, dissimilar from classical - Latin.

A sidenote is that "vulgar" stems from Latin "vulgaris", which describes the common people, next time you speak a vulgarity remind yourself of how "common" you are behaving.

To return, I personally speak some French alongside Spanish. They share similar lingustic rules, both having subject-object-verb order.

Similarly, their usage of postpositive adjective order rather than prepositive adjectives, as you may be familar with in English.

Postpositive adjectives coming after the noun which they modify, in English this would be such as "A house blue", in Spanish "Un casa azul", in French "Un maison bleu".

Similarities are also seen in both French and Spanish having a need for adjective-noun agreement in both gender and plurality.
